Latches and Flip-Flops

  • the building blocks of sequential circuits
  • gates can be built directly from transistors
  • latches can be built from gates
  • flip-flops can be built from latches.
  • An output of latches and flip-flops depends not only on the current inputs, but also on previous inputs and outputs. 
  • The difference between a latch and a flip-flop is that a latch does not have a clock signal, whereas a flip-flop always does.

Latches

  • How can we make a circuit out of gates that is not combinatorial?
    • feed-back - to create loops in the circuit diagrams
    • output values depend, indirectly, on themselves
    • If such feed-back is positive then the circuit tends to have stable states, and if it is negative the circuit will tend to oscillate.

  • Latches are asynchronous, which means that the output changes very soon after the input changes. 
  • Most computers today, on the other hand, are synchronous, which means that the outputs of all the sequential circuits change simultaneously to the rhythm of a global clock signal.

Flip-Flops

  • Level-triggered vs. Edge-triggered

    • Level-triggered

      • level-sensitive

      • input signals may affect the output during the entire interval when the clock signal is valid

    • Edge-triggered

      • edge-sensitive

      • input signals may affect the output only during the transition moment of the clock signal -- i.e., output changes only as a result of clock transition
